Musical start

The first things I remember music wise is what my dad used to play. He had some songs that he play on the guitar and sang and then there were some songs he played on the stereo. One song he use to play on the guitar is Johnny Cash - Sugartime, I think I remember it since it's a quite simple song. He used to play Jeff Healey - Jambalaya on the stereo, and is still doing that for my niece and nephew, while dancing around with them. There was also a lot of Beatles and Elvis Presley playing in the house. The two first records I got were by Bryan Adams and Michael Jackson and I had a few of each artist and I should have been about 7 years old at that time. I've just selected 4 songs, in case you wouldn't have heard of either of those bands/artists ;)
The Beatles-Hey Jude
Elivs Presley - (Marie's the Name) His Latest Flame
Bryan Adams - Can't Stop This Thing We Started
Michael Jackson - Black Or White

A couple of years later I come about a punk band, which got really big in Sweden (Dia Psalma). That started my musical venture into punk rock and skate punk, with Millencolin and Green Day as two of my favorites. Lastly when visiting my cousin I found that she had a Metallica record, which I started listening to, thinking that was too hard rock for me but liked Nothing Else Matters and Unforgiven and that then made me like all of their songs. I've been a fan since then, but am not listening to them that often anymore

Americana

I have claimed that I like country, but too be fair it's more americana music in general, with some artists who will be counted towards the country genre As I stated above my dad used to play Johnny Cash to me at a young age, and also had a few other record in that genre, e.g. Merle Haggard. That's probably one reason why I like that kind of music, but I more or less started listening to it 10 years or so ago. I used to listen to Dixie Chicks when riding my bike to work when I lived in California, with Travelin Soldier as one of the favorites.
